Chionin Temple

Chionin is the head temple of the Jodo sect of Japanese Buddhism.
The temple has spacious grounds and large bulding.

At the center of Chionin is an open area with stone paths connecting the temple's main buildings.
The massive Miedo Hall houses the temple's principle object of worship: a statue of the priest Honen, who founded the Jodo sect.
Nearby is the Amidado Hall, which houses a statue of Amida Buddha, the most important Buddha in the Jodo sect.
